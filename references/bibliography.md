## Cryptography & Hash Functions

1. National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST). *FIPS PUB 180-4, Secure Hash Standard (SHS)*. August 2015.

2. Daemen, J., & Rijmen, V. (2002). *The Design of Rijndael: AES - The Advanced Encryption Standard*. Springer.

3. Bertoni, G., Daemen, J., Peeters, M., & Van Assche, G. (2011). *The Keccak Reference*. Version 3.0.

4. Bernstein, D. J. (2009). *Cost analysis of hash collisions: Will quantum computers make SHARCS obsolete?*

## Cryptanalysis

5. Kelsey, J., & Schneier, B. (2004). *Second Preimages on n-bit Hash Functions for Much Less than 2ⁿ Work*.

6. Wang, X., Yin, Y. L., & Yu, H. (2005). *Finding Collisions in the Full SHA-1*. In Advances in Cryptology–CRYPTO 2005 (pp. 17-36). Springer Berlin Heidelberg.

## Ancient Scripts & Linguistics

7. Yale University. *Voynich Manuscript*. Beinecke Rare Book & Manuscript Library.

8. Parpola, A. (1994). *Deciphering the Indus Script*. Cambridge University Press.

9. Mahadevan, I. (1977). *The Indus Script: Texts, Concordance and Tables*. Archaeological Survey of India.

## Side-Channel Attacks

10. Kocher, P., Jaffe, J., & Jun, B. (1999). *Differential Power Analysis*. In Advances in Cryptology—CRYPTO'99 (pp. 388-397). Springer.

11. Agrawal, D., et al. (2003). *The EM Side-Channel(s)*. In Cryptographic Hardware and Embedded Systems—CHES 2002 (pp. 29-45). Springer.

## Quantum Computing

12. Grover, L. K. (1997). *Quantum Mechanics Helps in Searching for a Needle in a Haystack*. Physical Review Letters, 79(2), 325.

13. Boothby, K., et al. (2023). *Next-Generation Annealing Machines*. Nature Physics.
